Clients

Below is a list of some projects I've been working on during my employment at iris worldwide. Some of the sites below can't be linked to because they were promotions that ran for a short period. The latest projects start at the top.

MINI, Aftersales

ShowHide details

The MINI Aftersales project was one of my latest, and most enjoyable projects to work on. We were tasked to create a vehicle registration lookup and display a list of services availble for upgrade or renew if they qualify.

The project had to fit into MINI's existing site, which meant we had to include it as an iFrame. However it's likely that the project will be included natively in future revisions of the MINI site.

MINI Aftersales was created in mind of all devices, not just desktop.

Technologies include:

  • Sass/CSS

  • HTML 5

  • jQuery/Javascript

  • Microsoft .net

Due to the time and resource constraints, the project only works with Javascript enabled.

KIKO, Clic108 promotion

ShowHide details

Italian cosmetics company Kiko, instructed us to develop a Facebook tab for a short running competition in which users could fill in their contact details then choose a destination of their choice for a weekend holiday. The competition was open for about fifteen European countries.

One struggle we had was that iOS7 was released on the day before the competition launced. The issue was that Safari included swiping left/right to navigate, which conflicted with our colour palette swiping mechanism. We came to the conclusion that using navigation buttons instead of swiping solved the issue. Not the greatest solution, but the best solution in the time we had to deliver the project.

Technologies include:

  • CSS

  • HTML 5

  • jQuery & BXSlider (jQuery plugin)

  • Microsoft .net (for localised content)

Reckitt Benkeiser, Durex

ShowHide details

Durex was a challenging site build. Originally we were tasked at building their global site, with up to six microsites/interactive game sites, all of which would work responsively, be backward compatible with IE6, not to mention be localised.

However multiple problems arose such as, the creative director deciding that the whole look-and-feel should change half-way through the build (which affected both build and QA), the client (who were made up of the UK, Global, and North American division) being confused on what we were actually delivering, and the North American division wanting their site live before the agreed date. Regardless of internal and external problems, we managed to deliver a stable release on the day that was agreed.

In retrospect the site should have been rebuilt rather than added to, when the creative director requested fundamental changes.

Technologies include:

  • CSS & animation

  • HTML 5

  • jQuery/Javascript

  • Microsoft .net
